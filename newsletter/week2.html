UQ Mars Newsletter - Week 2

Fellow robot connoisseurs,

We hope you're all doing well and enjoying the start of another exciting year with UQ MARS.

Firstly, we want to say a huge thank you to everyone who attended our welcome night. It was fantastic to see so many familiar and new faces, and we hope you found it as inspiring and informative as we did.

This week same time, same place is our "Making Robots Think Faster" talk from Master Raghav Mishra (Raggy). Connecting hardware-accelerated video gaming to high speed robotic movements, make sure to be in 46-243 this Tuesday or risk being hit by some serious fomo.

Also, if you haven't already, don't forget to join our Discord community, where you can connect with fellow members, ask questions, and stay up-to-date with all the latest MARS news and events.

In this issue, we're thrilled to officially introduce MARS's industry partners, River City Labs (RCL) and Board of Professional Engineers Queensland (BPEQ). As some of you may already know, RCL is a hub providing tech startups the resources to go global , while BPEQ is a professional engineering registration body that promotes excellence and innovation in the engineering industry. We're proud to have these esteemed organizations as our partners, and we can't wait to work with them to keep making MARS awesome.

So, grab a drink, get comfortable, and read on to find out more about RCL, BPEQ, and some more exciting upcoming events happening in the world of MARS. Upcoming Events

  • Making Robots Think Faster

    Location: 46-243 - Andrew N. Liveris Building, Collaborative Room
    Time: Tuesday, 28 Feb 2023 at 6pm
    Type: Technical event
    Difficulty: Moderate
    Raghav will be talking about how we can use specialised computer hardware, especially GPUs that are common in gaming, to speed up how robots perceive and plan in the world efficiently.

    He will be briefly introducing:

    - CPU vs specialised hardware
    - GPUs and parallel execution
    - The current hardware options (Intel/NVIDIA/AMD GPUs, FPGAs) and the software scene (CUDA, OpenCL, SYCL)
    - Examples of how simple hardware accelerated code can be written
    - How you could get start off and get involved

    There's no prerequisites to understanding the talk! It would be beneficial to have some knowledge of basic computer hardware and simple C programming, but Raghav will explain everything.
